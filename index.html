Welcome to Project DataREACH!

Mission Statement

As data science continues to grow as an integral part of life in the 21 st century, its applications around the world continue to diversify and change the status quo. Our mission is to harness the powerful applications of machine learning and predictive analytics to make a positive impact on medicine in developing nations. Through the training of medical professionals on the fundamentals of data science as well as assisting in the creation of advanced analytics pipelines via machine learning, we hope to make groundbreaking strides in integrating AI in medicine in developing nations.

Guyana 2015


Started in August 2015 with founder Vikash Singh taking a three week trip to Guyana fostering connections with and giving presentations to hospitals (including Georgetown Public Hospital Corp, Balwant Singh Hospital, and Hanoman Medical Center), universities, and government members (received commendation from Guyana Ministry of Health/NCI) about how predictive analytics can start to be implemented/the advantages of predictive analysis/machine learning when applied to medicine, especially disease diagnosis via clinical data sets. For this initiative, he was commended by the nation’s Ministry of Health and National Cancer Institute as they saw the potential that such a program could have.

Cameroon 2016


In the summer of 2016 Project DataREACH expanded with a project in Cameroon thanks to a collaboration with Songhai Labs, which is global Knowledge Broker Platform for the African Continent with the ambition to positively transform African societies by sharing knowledge and innovations across countries and across sectors. We are currently working on a proposal to obtain some data from medical entities in Cameroon to begin looking into ways in which machine learning pipelines can usefully be created. We are also planning remote basic training sessions to enable motivated medical professionals in Cameroon to apply data science techniques to data they have access to and begin discussion of how these skills will impact the nation long term.

Faculty Advisors

Omolola Ogunyemi
Advisor
Lola Ogunyemi is a computer scientist and biomedical informatics researcher. Her research interests include computerized medical decision support, reasoning under uncertainty, 3D graphics and visualization, and machine learning. Her current research focuses on developing and evaluating novel computerized decision support systems for different biomedical domains, including diabetes, trauma, breast cancer, depression, chronic kidney disease, and HIV. She is currently working on a National Library of Medicine-funded R01 grant to explore diabetic retinopathy prediction from clinical data using different machine learning strategies. She has been principal investigator on a National Library of Medicine-funded R01 study of computerized decision support for penetrating trauma, and on a National Cancer Institute-funded R03 study of individualized breast cancer risk prediction using Bayesian networks. She is currently Director of Charles Drew University's Center for Biomedical Informatics and a co-leader of the UCLA CTSI's biomedical informatics program. She is also an Associate Adjunct Professor of Radiological Sciences in the David Geffen School of Medicine at UCLA with the Medical Imaging Informatics group.

Dr. Ogunyemi holds an undergraduate degree in Computer Science from Barnard College, New York, and an M.S.E, and Ph.D. in Computer and Information Science from the University of Pennsylvania. Before moving to Charles Drew University to become the Director of the Center for Biomedical Informatics, Dr. Ogunyemi was a biomedical informatics faculty member in the Department of Radiology at Brigham and Women's Hospital and Harvard Medical School from 1999 until 2007. She was also a member of the affiliated faculty in the Harvard-MIT Division of Health Sciences and Technology from 2003 until 2007. She served as a faculty member in the Boston-area National Library of Medicine-funded biomedical informatics fellowship training program (1999-2007).

She has taught graduate level biomedical informatics courses in the Harvard-MIT Division of Health Sciences and Technology, at UCLA, at CDU, and short courses on informatics at the University of Natal, Durban, South Africa. She served as an editorial board member of the journal Computers in Biology and Medicine from 2007 until 2013. She served on the National Library of Medicine's Biomedical Library and Informatics Review Committee study section from 2003-2007 and on the National Library of Medicine's Literature Selection and Technical Review Committee from 2010-2014 as a member and as chair (2013-2014). She is currently an editorial board member for the Journal of Biomedical Informatics (2015 - 2018) and a member of the Agency for Healthcare Research and Quality's Health Information Technology Research study section (2016 - 2019).
